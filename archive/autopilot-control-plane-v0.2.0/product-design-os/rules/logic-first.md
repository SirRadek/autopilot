# Logic First

Default priority order:

```text
purpose > workflow > data > roles > validation > usability > performance > design
```

Marketing, portfolio, and creative projects may raise design priority, but
design still must serve the goal and critical user action.

## Required Analysis

- What are we building?
- Who uses it?
- What is the main goal?
- What is the critical action?
- What content, data, products, or services are involved?
- What must not fail?
- What should be avoided?
- How important are logic, design, motion, performance, and accessibility?
- How will we know the result is good?

## Defaults By Type

Internal systems prioritize roles, permissions, states, workflows, validation,
auditability, keyboard use, error states, loading states, and empty states.

Ecommerce prioritizes product clarity, pricing, trust, cart logic, checkout,
variants, shipping, payment, mobile performance, and conversion.

Dashboards prioritize data clarity, filters, decisions, alerts, export,
drilldown, readable charts, and source freshness.

Marketing and portfolio pages prioritize clear positioning, CTA, visual
hierarchy, proof, case studies, memorability, and mobile readability.
